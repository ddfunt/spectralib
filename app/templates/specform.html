How to use this page

This app is designed to aid in the selection of a frequency band for rotational spectroscopy. One of the most difficult parts about choosing a frequency band is trying to select a band that is capable of measuring all of the molecules that may be in your experiment.

Select the molecule(s) of interest from the list below (multiple with ctrl+click). Fill in the form to specify the parameters that will best match your desired experiments.

To get a general overview fill keep the resolution elements at ~1. The resulting plot will be downsampled to allow for easy comparison of the locations of the strongest lines. You may then reduce the bandwidth or increase the resolution elements to get a better picture of local line density. Actual experiments have a resolution of ~10000 per GHz for room temperature experiments and ~50000 per GHz for jet cooled spectra under 60GHz.

When complete a stick style spectrum of each molecule will be displayed where the intensity in indicated by the color. Intensities are normalized by default, such that the most intense line of each spectrum is scaled to that of the most intense spectrum. If you prefer to view the relative intensities between spectra deselect the normalize option.
